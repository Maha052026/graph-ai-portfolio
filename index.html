Focus areas

What this course connects to

  • Using AI tools to organize research, writing, and communication workflows.
  • Creating polished, accessible outputs for academic and professional settings.
  • Exploring responsible automation in a way that supports quality and clarity.

About the course

The course is an 8-week live cohort program focused on no-code AI tools for responsible task automation, with weekly workshops, live help sessions, community interaction, feedback, and a final showcase.

1

Websites, Documents & Presentations

Learn to generate structured outputs for research communication and build simple websites with no-code tools.

  • LLMs and popular providers
  • Markdown, LaTeX, and HTML outputs
  • Research websites with ChatGPT Canvas and Replit
2

AI-Automated Data Analysis

Use AI tools to process data, manipulate files, and produce transparent, ethical automated reports.

  • Data analysis with Code Interpreter
  • Automated dashboards and visualizations
  • Spreadsheet automations
3

GenAI for Research & Text Analysis

Support literature search, text analysis, knowledge synthesis, and information organization.

  • Efficient literature reviews
  • Text classification and sentiment analysis
  • Automated summarization workflows
4

Custom GPTs and Chatbots

Build tailored chat experiences for research assistance, knowledge management, and task support.

  • RAG systems and context windows
  • Custom GPTs for specific use cases
  • Botpress workflows
5

AI for Task Automation

Design responsible automation for repetitive research tasks and app-to-app workflows.

  • Agent-based systems
  • Email response automations
  • Browser control agents
6

GenAI for Communication & Outreach

Develop transparent methods for summaries, diagrams, video abstracts, and research communication.

  • Audience-specific summaries
  • Scientific figures and diagrams
  • Content scheduling for outreach
7

Capstone Project & Showcase

Refine a final AI solution, receive feedback, and present the work in a public showcase.

  • Progress check and peer feedback
  • Final presentation
  • Public Q&A session
∞

Rubric & course rhythm

The course emphasizes regular participation, practical assignments, and a capstone project.

  • Online quizzes: 15%
  • Workshop assignments: 35%
  • Attendance & participation: 10%
  • Final project: 40%
